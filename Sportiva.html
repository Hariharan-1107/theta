Power Lifting

Powerlifting is a strength sport where competitors perform three lifts – squat, bench press, and deadlift – with the heaviest weight possible.

The rules vary slightly depending on the organization, but in general, each competitor has three attempts to complete each lift, and the highest weight successfully lifted in each lift is added together to determine their total score.Lifters must follow specific rules on technique and form, and each lift is judged by three referees who decide whether the lift was successful or not based on strict criteria. The lifter with the highest total score in their weight class wins

Dead Hang

Dead hang is a game where players hang from a horizontal bar or elevated object with both hands for as long as possible. The player must not touch the ground or any other object during the game and cannot receive assistance or support from others.

The game ends when the player can no longer maintain their grip on the bar or touches the ground with any part of their body. The winner is the player who hangs from the bar for the longest amount of time/125 seconds.

Arm wrestling

Arm wrestling is a one-on-one physical competition between two individuals, where each person attempts to pin the other person’s arm to a designated surface.

The game starts with both participants placing their elbow on a table or other surface, and gripping each other’s hand. The objective is to use arm strength and technique to force the opponent’s hand down to the table, or touch the designated surface with their hand. The winner is the person who successfully pins their opponent’s arm or hand to the table.

Musical chair

To start, you’ll need a few chairs set up in a circle or line, with one fewer chair than the number of players. When the music starts, everyone walks or dances around the chairs, and when it stops, players must quickly sit in a chair.

The player left standing is out and one chair is removed. This continues until there is only one chair and one player left, who is the winner.

One over cricket

In a one over cricket game, each team faces a single over of six balls. The batting team aims to score as many runs as possible, while the fielding team tries to limit the runs scored and take wickets.

The batsman can be dismissed in the same ways as in regular cricket, such as being caught out, bowled, or run out. If the ball is hit out of the playing area, it’s considered a six, and if it bounces before crossing the boundary, it’s a four. The team with the most runs at the end of their over wins the game. It’s a quick and exciting format of cricket that can be played in a short amount of time.

lucky corner

Lucky corner is a simple and fun game often played at events and parties. To start, four corners of the room are designated as lucky corners, and one person is chosen to be “it.”

The players then close their eyes and count to ten while “it” chooses one of the lucky corners. Once the counting is done, the players try to guess which corner is the lucky one. If a player chooses the correct corner, they remain in the game, and “it” chooses again. If a player chooses an incorrect corner, they are out, and the game continues until only one player is left.
